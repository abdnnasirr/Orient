---
name: write-brain
description: Use when filing durable knowledge into the brain (the markdown vault), to put it on its own page, create or update rather than duplicate, and link it into the web.
---

<the-point>

write-brain files a durable thing into the brain so it compounds. Three moves: **find its page** (the right sector + type), **create or update** (one coherent account, no history), **link it** (wikilinks + index.md + log.md).

- **The brain grows as many small linked pages,** one per thing, never a few sprawling files.
- **Write in his first person, his voice:** a page reads as him thinking ("I do X, not Y"), framed whole before the parts, never a third-person report about him. It is his own mind handed back, plain and clear.
- **The vault's own schema is the law:** its AGENTS.md defines the sectors, the page types, and the frontmatter. Follow it.

</the-point>

<find-its-page>

**A page is per nameable thing,** placed by the vault's two axes: its **sector** (how the user navigates) and its **type** (entity, concept, source, or synthesis).

- **A new entity or concept earns its own page.** A smaller fact or detail goes onto the page of the thing it concerns, not a page of its own.
- **One thing, one page.** If it spans sectors, pick the truer home and link from the others.
- **A detail graduates to its own page** once the weekly clean-brain sees it recur enough to deserve one.

</find-its-page>

<create-or-update>

**If the thing already has a page, update it; if it has none, create it.** Never write a second page for something that already has one.

- **Updating means the page stays one coherent account of what is true now,** no history narration: work the new in.
- **Where the new contradicts the old,** keep the truer version and note what changed.
- **Pages stay right-sized:** the weekly clean-brain splits an overgrown one and promotes a recurring sub-idea to its own page.

</create-or-update>

<link-it>

**Connect the page with [[wikilinks]] to its neighbors** so nothing is an orphan and the brain reads as a graph: an entity to the concepts it touches, a synthesis to its sources, a project to its findings.

- **A link to a page that does not exist yet is fine;** it marks one worth writing.
- **Keep the two navigation files current:** update the page's one-line entry in index.md, and append a dated line to log.md.

</link-it>
